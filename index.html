So what does this mean in reality?

The recycling system in the US is not failing because of a lack of infrastructure alone. It is failing at the point of disposal — the moment each person decides what goes in which bin. $6.5 billion worth of recyclable material is lost to landfill every year. The majority of that loss happens at the household level, before a single truck is dispatched. Better sorting at the point of disposal is the highest leverage intervention available. The data is clear on that. What happens next depends on whether awareness translates into habit change.
